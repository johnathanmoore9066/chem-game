# ⚛️ Element Fusion

Welcome to **Element Fusion**, the browser-based chemistry sandbox where players drag, drop, and snap elements together until something either stabilizes or triggers a deeply concerning cosmic event. 

Think of it as *2048* meets actual chemical valences, designed to help students transition from rote memorization to organic, kinetic discovery. No clunky drop-down menus, no digital worksheets—just pure, system-driven **dynamic coalescence**.

Play the live game for free at: **https://elementfusion.org**

---

## 🧠 Big Brain Energy (How It Works)

We built a live reactive canvas backed by a strict data engine. If a player drops two Hydrogens and an Oxygen into the reaction zone, they don't just sit there looking at each other awkwardly. The engine constantly evaluates the structural properties of the canvas. If the recipe is valid, the nodes immediately snap together, update the player's discovered ledger, and remain on the canvas to cascade into even higher-tier mixtures. 

Yes, that means they can make salt, make water, and watch them combine into saline solution without resetting their workspace. It's chemistry as a fluid, logical puzzle.

## 👩‍🏫 For the Educators: We Need Your Recipes!

Are your students looking for a specific polyatomic ion that isn't in the game yet? Do you want to see complex organic chains, weird isotopes, or specific AP Chemistry compounds added to the sandbox? We want this ledger to be massive, and we need your expertise to expand the database.

You can contribute new elements, compounds, or mixtures to our data file in one of two ways:

### Option A: The Savvy GitHub Route (Pull Requests)
If you know your way around a repository, feel free to fork this project, drop your additions directly into the data file, and submit a **Pull Request**. We will review it, test the valences, and merge it into production.

### Option B: The "Just Email Me" Route
Not a Git wizard? No problem. Drop an email to **contribute@elementfusion.org** with your suggested compound. To make sure it fits directly into our backend matrix, please format your suggestion using bullet points for each piece of data, like this:

* **ID:** `methane` (lowercase, unique identifier)
* **Name:** `Methane`
* **Formula:** `CH₄`
* **Kind:** `compound` (can be element, compound, or mixture)
* **Starter:** `false` (set to true only for the core starting elements)
* **Category:** `organic`
* **Tags:** `organic`, `molecule`, `fuel`
* **Blurb:** `The simplest hydrocarbon — one carbon, four hydrogens, perfect tetrahedral symmetry.`
* **Use:** `Natural gas: heating, cooking, electricity generation.`
* **Fun Fact:** `Methane is actually completely odorless! The "gas smell" we associate with it is mercaptan, a sulfur compound intentionally added by utility companies so human noses can easily detect leaks. (And no, space does not smell like a fart.)`
