A free teaching tool

Element ⚛ Fusion

A hands-on chemistry sandbox. Drag elements into each other and discover real compounds through real reactions — stellar fusion in the core, bench chemistry everywhere else. Every discovery comes with the actual chemistry: what you made, why it works, and where it shows up in the world.

  • Free, for everyone. Built for classrooms, curious kids, and anyone who ever wondered why water is H₂O.
  • No accounts. No tracking. No data collected. Your progress saves only to your own browser — nothing ever leaves your device.
  • Open chemistry. All reactions live in one readable data file — teachers can extend it with their own curriculum. Suggest a reaction →
Frequently asked questions
Is this actually free?
Yes — no paid tiers, no locked content, no catch. If it helps your classroom, there's an optional "buy me a coffee" link, but that's it.
Do students need accounts?
No accounts, no sign-ups, no logins. Progress saves to the student's own browser and never leaves their device — zero COPPA/FERPA concerns.
Does it need to be installed?
No. Open the URL, play. Works in any modern desktop browser; no download or IT approval required.
Can I add reactions from my curriculum?
Yes. All chemistry lives in one readable file. Fill out a template and email it in — or submit a GitHub pull request if you're comfortable with that. Both options are under the 🧪 "Suggest a reaction" button above.
What grade level is this for?
Primarily middle and high school chemistry, but the stellar nucleosynthesis track works well for intro physics too. Any curious learner is welcome.

This project runs on goodwill. If it helps your classroom, — entirely optional, always appreciated.

Achievements

Suggest a reaction

Teaching a reaction the game doesn't have? It's built to be extended — all of the chemistry lives in one data file, and adding to it takes one filled-out template. Two ways to send yours in:

✉️ Email it in no account needed

Send a filled-out template to johnathanmoore@elementfusion.org. That link pre-fills the template for you — or and paste it into any email.

🔀 Submit it yourself via GitHub

Comfortable with GitHub — or willing to try? The contributor guide in the README has the full template, the house rules, and a never-used-GitHub-before walkthrough: fork, edit data.js, open a pull request.

Every submission is reviewed for chemical accuracy. Complete templates get plugged in as-is — and you get credited in the next update.
